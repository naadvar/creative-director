# Long-term strategy — marketing, funding, roadmap

_Synthesized 2026-07-01 from a 4-lens strategy panel (GTM, monetization/funding,
product roadmap, moat/defensibility), each grounded in the codebase + web research._

## Thesis

Wedge: become the honest craft critic for short-form FITNESS creators — a frames-grounded read that names one timestamped fix, verifies with the OLD-vs-NEW revision loop whether the fix actually landed, and says nothing rather than fabricate. Engine: every read and re-check compounds a proprietary outcome dataset (NoteFeedback labels + revision verdicts: change_type x niche x edit -> fixed/still-there) that makes the read measurably better in-niche, which drives word-of-mouth in fitness creator communities, ShareCard distribution, and retention via Creator DNA + push — no paid acquisition needed to find signal. Endgame: expand the same playbook niche by niche (fitness -> food -> travel -> TikTok) into the default 'second opinion editor' for short-form, monetized at $9.99/$19.99-49 subscriptions, bootstrapped to ~$2.5-5K MRR by month 12, raising outside capital only if a coach/agency B2B2C motion outgrows solo bandwidth.

## Launch window (the ~4 weeks around App Store approval)

1. WEEK 1 (in review — prep, ~15h): (1) Set the Resend key — creative_director/notify/email.py is inert-by-default and needs only the key + a verified domain; wire 2 emails: welcome and 'your read is ready' with deep link. (2) Ship revision-loop telemetry from day one: log every [change_type, niche, prior_issue, re-check?, verdict] tuple — this data cannot be collected retroactively and is the moat. (3) Add the post-read Share CTA routing to the already-built ShareCard.tsx (currently undiscovered by users). (4) Landing page: add 'Why no virality promises' positioning section + email-capture for launch notify (target 50-100 signups from personal X/Discord). (5) Operational hygiene Apple requires: support@ address (forwarding is fine) + abuse-report path (IOS_SUBMISSION.md already scopes it). (6) Draft launch posts (X thread, 20-30s TikTok of you uploading your own reel) and shoot a 2-min demo video. (7) DM 10-15 fitness creators (1K-50K followers): free access, no strings, lined up for approval day.

2. WEEK 2 (approval day + 48h): Within 24h of approval: founder X thread (the honesty stance, a real read screenshot, App Store link), the TikTok demo ('tired of tools that promise viral? this one just tells you the truth'), email the launch list, ONE genuine post to r/NewTubers leading with the web link. Personally onboard every single signup (DM/email each one). Watch like a hawk: suppression rate, first-read completion time, helpful/not-quite votes. Realistic goal: 100-200 signups, 25-50 weekly uploaders — NOT the 500-1000 DAU the marketing lens projected; you have zero audience and that number would require a channel you don't have.

3. WEEK 3: Product Hunt launch (1 day of effort, day 3-4 of the week; expect a modest 100-300 visits at 2026's ~10% feature rate — treat as a backlink + credibility artifact, not the plan). Ship push notifications ('Your read is ready' -> deep link to /video/{id}) via Capacitor + OneSignal — the read is an async 1-2 min job, so this is the single highest-leverage retention feature and it should land while the launch cohort is still active. Start the 2x/week build-in-public cadence: craft reads of YOUR OWN reels and consented early users' reels only.

4. WEEK 4: Convert the fitness-creator DMs into 10+ active users; run 5 recorded user interviews (watch them read their read — where do they nod, where do they squint). Collect 3-5 real testimonials for the landing page. Stand up the KPI sheet you will run the company on: Weekly Active Uploaders, D7/D30 retention, reads/user/week, helpful%, suppression rate, revision re-check count. First Day-7 re-upload nudge email goes out. Go/no-go checkpoint: if D7 retention of the launch cohort is under ~15%, stop all outbound and fix the first-session experience before spending another hour on growth.

## 0-3 months

- MONTH 1: launch window above. Ends with: 150-250 signups, 40-60 Weekly Active Uploaders, D7 >= 20-25%, 200+ NoteFeedback labels, 50+ revision verdicts, 3-5 testimonials. All marketing is founder-led and free: 2 posts/week + 2h/week community routine (r/NewTubers Feedback Friday participation with genuine critique, 2-3 fitness-creator Discords, X engagement) — capped at ~6h/week total so product time survives.
- MONTH 2: Retention build-out on existing rails: deepen Creator DNA (streaks, fastest-improving dimension, per-niche corpus baseline from the 6,796-reel corpus) + 'Weekly Craft Pulse' Resend digest for users with 3+ reads. Ship the Read Transparency export (what the model saw, gate verdict, suppression reason) and start publishing suppression stats — it converts 'honest' from a claim into an auditable fact and is your best marketing artifact. Run the 1-2 day multi-vendor VLM A/B (DeepInfra Qwen vs the already-wired Claude fallback vs one more) on ~50 QA reels so a DeepInfra pricing email can never kill the product. Targets: 200-350 signups cumulative, 75-100 WAU, D30 >= 15%.
- MONTH 2-3: Dark-launch billing infrastructure (RevenueCat wrapping StoreKit2 on iOS + Stripe on web; enroll in Apple Small Business Program for the 15% cut) — build it now so the paywall flip later is a config change, but DO NOT show a paywall. Begin the fitness accuracy sprint: 20-30 recorded fitness-creator sessions, retune _VERIFY_FIX_SYSTEM and the grounding gate for fitness-specific patterns (e.g., before/after payoffs are backloaded by design), ship 'now better at fitness.'
- MONTH 3: Android build via the same Capacitor codebase + Codemagic, GATED on iOS D7 >= 25% — reach lever only after retention proof, since Android roughly doubles organic install surface but converts worse. First paid experiment allowed ONLY if D7 >= 25%: $300-500 total on 10-15 nano fitness creators (free access + $30-50 for 1 honest post) with tracked links — this replaces the marketing lens's $2K month-1 seeding budget, which would be spent before you know whether acquired users stick.
- MONTH 3 EXIT CRITERIA (the bar for everything that follows): 150-200 Weekly Active Uploaders, D7 >= 25%, D30 >= 18-20%, >= 1.5 reads/user/week, helpful% >= 60%, 800+ NoteFeedback labels, 300+ revision verdicts. If WAU < 50 at month 3: freeze all feature work, run 20 user interviews, and treat it as a positioning/channel problem, not a product-depth problem.

## 3-12 months

- MONTH 4 (or whenever the trigger hits — 200 WAU AND D30 >= 20% AND helpful% >= 65%): flip the paywall per the pricing decision. Expect 2-4% free->paid over 90 days (freemium benchmark 2.6-5.1%): at 200 WAU that is a symbolic $60-120 MRR — the point is proving the conversion lever exists, not revenue.
- MONTH 4-6: Double down on the fitness wedge: 50 cumulative creator interviews, fitness-tuned prompts shipped, publish first transparency stats post ('across N reads we suppressed X% rather than guess; Y% of re-checked fixes verified as fixed'). Scale the nano-creator seeding to ~$1K total ONLY for channels where month-3 cohorts showed D30 >= 20%. Community routine continues; it is the primary channel until something else demonstrably beats it.
- MONTH 6: TikTok upload support (craft_xray format_class extension + TikTok-aware pacing corpus via Apify public metadata) — gated on the fitness wedge holding (helpful% >= 65% sustained). This is the biggest TAM unlock but only worth it once the read has an earned reputation. Target: 300-400 WAU, $300-600 MRR.
- MONTH 6-9: Coach/agency pilot: hand-recruit 5 fitness coaches who review client reels; ship batch re-check (upload N old reels, parallel verdicts — compute cost ~$0.20-0.40/batch) and share-with-client links as the $49/mo Agency tier. This is the ARPU escalator: 5 coaches = $245/mo, equal to ~25 Pro subs. Android learnings folded in; localization still deferred.
- MONTH 9: FUNDING DECISION POINT (see funding_decision). Also declare niche #2 (food — closest corpus, fastest verifier portability) only if fitness helpful% >= 70% and WAU >= 400.
- MONTH 9-12: Publish the 'Creator Craft Report' from anonymized corpus + verdict data (most common fitness fix = X, creators who re-check 3+ times retain Y% better) — the PR narrative only you can write because only you have outcome data. Targets at month 12: 600-1,000 WAU, 60-120 paying (blended ~2.5-3% conversion + coaches), $2.5-5K MRR, <6% monthly paid churn. Fixed costs remain ~$10/mo + $99/yr + COGS (~$200-500/mo at this scale), so everything above ~$600 MRR is margin.

## Pricing decision

Freemium, flipped at month ~4 on trigger (200 WAU AND D30 >= 20% AND helpful% >= 65%), never at launch. FREE: 3 full reads per rolling 30 days; UNLIMITED revision re-checks on any read you already own (metering the verifier would both break the honesty brand and starve the moat dataset — re-checks are the labeled data); full Library history; Creator DNA basic trend. Worst-case free COGS is 3 x $0.08 = $0.24/user/month — 1,000 pure-free users cost under $250/mo, so generosity is nearly free. PRO $9.99/mo or $79/yr (34% discount): unlimited reads, Creator DNA depth (per-niche corpus benchmarks, streaks, trend charts), priority queue, Read Transparency CSV export. COACH/AGENCY $49/mo (ships month 6-9, only after observed coach demand): 5 client folders, batch parallel re-checks, share-with-client links. Rails: RevenueCat wrapping StoreKit2 on iOS + Stripe on web at identical sticker price; enroll in Apple Small Business Program (15% cut, you are nowhere near the $1M ceiling). Rationale: $9.99 matches the creator-tool band (TubeBuddy $12, CapCut $9.99, Submagic ~$20) without premium pretension; unit economics work at tiny scale ($9.99 - 15% - ~$0.50 COGS = ~$8 margin/sub/mo); RevenueCat's own 2026 data shows hard paywalls convert 5x better on session one but identical 1-year retention — and for a trust-positioned product with zero users, early friction costs you the users AND the labeled data that are the actual moat. Reject the Monetization lens's launch-day 3-reads-then-paywall: at its own projections it yields $30-40/mo by month 3 while suppressing the flywheel.

## Funding decision

BOOTSTRAP by default; the question re-opens at month 9 on triggers, not vibes. Resolving the direct conflict between lenses: the Monetization lens says the climate is hot and to raise $250-500K now; the Roadmap lens cites creator-economy funding collapsing from $807M/11 deals to $58M/9 deals. Whichever stat is right is irrelevant, because the raise-now case is built on traction that does not exist — its pitch cites '20-30 beta testers with strong NPS' and 'product-market fit already evident' when reality is founder + a couple of testers and zero WAU. Pitching that narrative is itself an honesty-positioning violation, and a raise now would cost ~3 months of the only scarce resource (founder evenings/weekends) for a priced-on-story round. Bootstrapping is trivially viable: fixed costs ~$10/mo + $99/yr, COGS $0.03-0.08/read; a $500 MRR business is already cash-positive. RAISE TRIGGERS — pursue $250-500K pre-seed (YC application is cheap optionality alongside) only when ALL of: (a) >= 500 WAU with D30 >= 25%; (b) >= $1.5K MRR growing >= 15% month-over-month with paid churn < 6%; (c) a specific capital-bound bottleneck exists — a coach/agency pipeline you cannot service solo, or a paid channel with proven CAC and < 3-month payback. If (a)-(b) hit but (c) does not, keep bootstrapping — capital without a deployment thesis just adds board pressure to chase virality features that break the brand. If month-9 metrics come in below half of these, neither raise nor quit-your-runway: stay nights-and-weekends and re-examine the wedge.

## North star

Weekly Active Uploaders (signed-in users with >= 1 completed read that week) — it is the one number that simultaneously drives retention, the labeled-data moat (every read/re-check is a training example), and future revenue. First-quarter weekly targets: Wk1 (launch): 25 -> Wk2: 50 (spike) -> Wk3: 45 (post-spike dip is expected, do not panic) -> Wk4: 55 -> Wk6: 70 -> Wk8: 90 -> Wk10: 115 -> Wk13: 150 (~10-15% w/w compounding after the spike). Guardrails that keep the north star honest: D7 retention >= 25%, fix-helpful rate >= 60%, and zero substantiated fabrication complaints — WAU bought at the cost of any of these is fake progress.

## Do NOT do

- Do not promise, imply, or market virality or view growth ANYWHERE — no 'grow your views' ASO keywords, no 'this fix took them viral' case studies, no before/after view-count content. Internal research says observable features don't predict views; the moment marketing contradicts that, the positioning is dead.
- Do not run famous creators' reels through the tool for content ('I ran [Creator]'s viral reel through an AI critic') as the Marketing lens suggests — consent/rights risk, and public unsolicited critique reads as a gotcha, which is off-brand for an honesty product. Own reels and consented users' reels only.
- Do not gate the revision verifier, grounding/suppression honesty, or existing Library behind the paywall — it is both brand suicide and starves the exact labeled dataset that is the moat. Charge for volume, depth, and convenience; never for truth.
- Do not ship any read-like output that bypasses the grounding gate — the Roadmap lens's 'lightweight checklist without grounding gate' would fabricate at exactly the moment a first-time user is deciding whether to trust you. If a quick-check mode ever ships, it passes the same gate or it does not ship.
- Do not raise pre-seed now on a traction narrative that does not exist (there are no '20-30 beta testers with strong NPS') — investors diligence it, and getting caught inflating traction while selling honesty is fatal.
- Do not flip the paywall before 200 WAU + D30 >= 20% — friction at zero-distribution kills the only scarce assets: users and their labels.
- Do not spend the Marketing lens's $2K influencer budget in month 1 — with no retention data you are paying to churn users. Cap at $300-500, and only after the launch cohort shows D7 >= 25%.
- Do not quote unverified market stats in your own marketing (the '$4.2B AI video feedback market,' 'Higgsfield $200M ARR' claims in the lens reports are unvetted) — for this brand specifically, every public number must be sourceable or it undermines the whole stance.
- Do not add virality-adjacent features (trending-sounds tracker, hook database, 'compare to top reels,' any score that ranks reels by predicted performance) — they are the competitors' thesis and re-anchor users to view-prediction, which the product deliberately refuses.
- Do not build referral incentives, team accounts, batch upload, public API, or localization before the retention curve proves (roughly: not before 200+ WAU / D30 >= 20%) — each is support debt and positioning dilution for a solo founder; forced referrals in particular make an honest product feel mercenary.
- Do not auto-ingest content from users' IG/TikTok accounts — stay upload-first; platform ToS enforcement is an existential, uncontrollable risk.
- Do not let support/abuse-report obligations slide (App Store Guidelines 1.2 / 5.1.1) — a professional support address and 24h response SLA are load-bearing for keeping the app listed, and naadvar@gmail.com as the public support contact should be replaced in week 1.

## Risks

- **Apple rejects or slow-walks the review (possibly flagging the deliberately-silent 'no read' behavior as broken UX)**
  - The web PWA at creative-director-psi.vercel.app is fully functional — the entire launch plan runs on web links regardless of the app's status; Android via the same Capacitor codebase is the pressure valve; support/abuse paths operational in week 1 remove the most common rejection ground; explain the suppression behavior in App Review notes as a feature, with a screenshot of the 'what looked good' fallback.
- **Cold start never ignites — zero audience means launch week produces 30 signups, not 300 (the single most likely failure mode)**
  - Founder-led content 2x/week + 2h/week genuine community participation in fitness creator spaces; personally onboard every one of the first 100 users; ShareCard + transparency artifacts give each user a reason to show the product. Tripwire: if WAU < 50 at month 3, freeze features and run 20 interviews — treat it as positioning/channel, not product depth.
- **One public fabrication — a hallucinated blind spot or a false 'Fixed' verdict screenshot — torches the honesty brand**
  - Keep the two-pass gate conservative (accept more silence, never loosen for engagement); the two-version verifier already never sees the new read's text; Read Transparency export + published suppression stats make errors auditable rather than scandalous; if it happens, ship a fix + public postmortem within 48h — the brand survives an owned mistake, not a covered one.
- **Incumbent copy: CapCut/Opus/Descript ships a 'craft critique' feature inside their distribution machine (12-18 month window per the moat analysis)**
  - Compound what they cannot copy: the revision-verdict outcome dataset (collected from day 1), fitness-niche verifier depth, and an honesty reputation that takes 6-12 months to earn and would contradict Opus's virality-score thesis. Do not compete on feature count.
- **DeepInfra sunsets or reprices Qwen-VL — single-vendor inference dependency**
  - Claude fallback is already wired (~2-3x cost, survivable at $0.10-0.25/read); run the 1-2 day multi-vendor A/B in month 1-2 so switching is one env var; keep prompts vendor-agnostic.
- **Solo-founder bandwidth collapse: marketing cadence + support + feature work + a day job's worth of hours don't fit in evenings/weekends**
  - Hard caps: ~6h/week marketing, one feature in flight at a time, the do_not_do list as a standing scope defense; automate everything ownable (email digests, KPI sheet); the AI-assisted velocity advantage only holds if scope stays narrow.
- **Monetization underwhelms — 2-3% freemium conversion on a few hundred WAU is double-digit dollars, and it feels like failure**
  - Pre-commit to the math: paywall revenue is not meaningful below ~500 WAU; COGS is so low that runway is never threatened; annual plans and the $49 coach tier are the ARPU levers; judge the model at month 9, not month 5.
- **Wrong wedge — fitness creators turn out not to care enough, or the read's fitness accuracy edge doesn't materialize**
  - Instrument helpful% and D30 BY NICHE from day 1; weekly creator interviews from week 2; be willing to switch the wedge to food or travel at month 4 based on per-niche numbers, not attachment — the playbook (niche corpus + tuned verifier + community) ports, only the target changes.

## Lens headlines (inputs to the synthesis)

- **Marketing / Go-to-Market**: Build a zero-paid launch around founder-led storytelling and the honest positioning, then sustain with community engagement in creator niches and built-in share mechanics.
- **Monetization + Funding Strategy**: A solo technical founder can bootstrap to $2-5K MRR within 12 months by launching a freemium model with conservative rate-limiting, then raise seed funding at $4M median valuation once hitting 100+ WAU with 3-5% paid conversion — but immediate funding may be faster given 2026's AI creator-tool climate and strong product-market validation already in-app.
- **Product Roadmap: Post-Approval Feature Prioritization**: Retention + Acquisition over monetization: push notifications and Creator DNA depth ship highest value on existing rails; Android ships for reach; paywall deferred until organic growth stabilizes.
- **Moat / Defensibility / Risk**: Creative Director's defensibility rests on three compounding assets (labeled feedback data, revision-verification dataset, honesty-as-brand) but faces immediate feature-copy risk from incumbents and platform dependency threats that require a niche-wedge strategy within 12 months.
