## 3. Before/after case studies from real users

The revision loop produces the perfect case-study artifact Ã¢â‚¬â€ a craft before/after with a machine-verified outcome Ã¢â‚¬â€ and it's the one content format competitors structurally cannot copy. **Hard rule: a case study is a craft before/after, never a performance before/after. No view counts, ever** (STRATEGY.md do-not-do list; one "this fix took them viral" post kills the positioning).

### Consent flow (the privacy posture makes this non-negotiable)
The App Store privacy label and review notes state uploads are private, never published, never shown to others. That default never changes in-product. Marketing use happens only via explicit, written, out-of-band opt-in:

1. **Trigger:** a user gets a "Fixed" verdict, or taps Helpful and says something positive in a DM/email. Those are the only people you ask.
2. **Ask** (DM/email, personal): use the template below. Never ask in-app; never make it a condition of anything.
3. **Scope tiers** the user picks from: (a) read screenshot only, no footage; (b) read + up to 3 frame stills; (c) read + a short reel excerpt. Attribution: named with handle, or anonymized ("a fitness creator, 12K followers").
4. **Approval pass:** they see the exact final asset before it posts. Revocable any time Ã¢â‚¬â€ you delete the post, no questions.
5. **Log it:** a consent sheet (name, handle, date, scope tier, asset link, revoked?). Five minutes of bookkeeping that protects the whole brand.

**Consent ask template:**
> "Hey [name] Ã¢â‚¬â€ that re-check verdict on your [reel topic] edit was exactly what I built the app for. Would you let me feature it as a short case study? Specifically: [scope tier].
