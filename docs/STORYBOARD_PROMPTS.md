# Nano Banana Pro storyboard prompts — launch video 1 ("I let an AI critique my reel")

Visual aids for the shoot: generate one frame per beat + a one-sheet storyboard
grid. These depict OUR app UI, so keep the style block consistent.

**Two tips that make Nano Banana Pro dramatically better here:**
1. **Feed it reference images.** Attach (a) a selfie of the person who'll be on
   camera and (b) a REAL screenshot of the app's read page along with the prompt —
   it will keep the face consistent across panels and copy the UI exactly instead
   of inventing one.
2. **Generate the grid first, then re-roll single panels** you don't like using
   the same style block — cheaper than re-rolling the whole sheet.

---

## MASTER STYLE BLOCK (paste at the top of every prompt)

Photorealistic vertical smartphone-video still, 9:16, shot on a phone front
camera, natural indoor lighting, authentic UGC aesthetic (NOT studio, NOT
stock-photo glossy). Subject: a young woman in her mid-20s, athletic wear,
long dark hair in a ponytail, in a small home gym with a mirror and a squat
rack softly out of focus behind her. The app shown on phone screens is
"Creative Director": near-black background (#0b0b10), white text, one accent
gradient from violet (#7c5cff) to cyan (#21c8ff), rounded cards, minimal dark
UI. All on-screen and overlay text must be crisp, correctly spelled, and
large enough to read at thumbnail size.

---

## BEAT 1 — the hook (0:00–0:02)

[MASTER STYLE BLOCK]
Close-up selfie framing: she looks slightly off-camera mid-thought, phone in
hand, eyebrows a touch skeptical, as if she just decided to do something
risky. Big bold white text overlay with subtle black outline, centered in the
upper third of the frame, occupying about 25% of image height, two lines:
"I let an AI critique my reel." and below it "No sugarcoating." The text is
the visual anchor; her expression sells apprehension. No app UI visible yet.
Slightly imperfect framing like a real selfie video frame-grab.

## BEAT 2 — the upload (0:02–0:05)

[MASTER STYLE BLOCK]
Full-frame phone screen recording (the phone UI fills the entire image): the
Creative Director app mid-analysis. Dark screen, centered glowing app icon
above the words "Reading your reel" in bold white, a thin animated gradient
progress bar (violet-to-cyan), and below it a vertical checklist stepper with
five items: "Uploading" (checked, gradient check circle), "Reading every
frame" (active, pulsing outlined circle), "Writing the read", "Fact-checking
it", "Final touches" (dim, unchecked). Small caption text at the bottom of
the screen: "Watching every frame, not just the thumbnail." A small white
text overlay at the top of the image reads: "it watches every single frame".

## BEAT 3 — the verdict (0:05–0:11)

[MASTER STYLE BLOCK]
Full-frame phone screenshot of the app's read page. At top, small uppercase
gray label "CRAFT READ". Below it a large white verdict sentence: "Clean,
confident demo — but your best moment is buried at 0:14." Then a highlighted
rounded card with a violet-cyan gradient border glow, containing the
uppercase gradient label "FIX THIS FIRST" and the text: "0:07 — Your caption
flashes for under a second. Hold it 2 full seconds, lower third, bigger
type." Below the card, a smaller section titled "What's working" with two
green check marks and short lines: "Side-on angle shows depth clearly" and
"Consistent lighting throughout". Everything legible, dark UI, realistic
mobile app typography.

## BEAT 4 — the reaction (0:11–0:16) — THE MONEY SHOT

[MASTER STYLE BLOCK]
Selfie close-up: she stares at the camera deadpan-offended, one eyebrow
raised, mouth slightly open in reluctant agreement — the exact face of
"...okay, that's annoyingly true." She holds the phone half-raised in the
lower corner of frame, its screen showing a blurred glimpse of the dark read
UI with the gradient card. Text overlay in the lower third, bold white with
black outline: "okay… that's annoyingly true". Warm natural window light,
authentic imperfect selfie framing, candid energy.

## BEAT 5 — the close (0:16–0:22)

[MASTER STYLE BLOCK]
Medium selfie framing, she speaks directly to camera with a half-smile,
mid-sentence gesture with one hand. Text overlay centered in the lower
third, two lines, bold white with black outline: "It doesn't tell you how to
go viral." and below "It just tells you the truth." Composition feels like
the final beat of a reel: confident, direct eye contact, ready for a hard
cut. Behind her the home gym is slightly darker, evening light.

## ONE-SHEET STORYBOARD GRID (the printable shoot card)

[MASTER STYLE BLOCK]
A clean storyboard sheet on a white background: five 9:16 panels in a row,
each with a hand-drawn-style frame number and a timing label underneath
("0:00–0:02", "0:02–0:05", "0:05–0:11", "0:11–0:16", "0:16–0:22").
Panel 1: close-up of the woman looking skeptical, overlay text "I let an AI
critique my reel. No sugarcoating."
Panel 2: full phone screen of a dark app analyzing a video, headline
"Reading your reel", gradient progress bar, five-step checklist.
Panel 3: full phone screen of the finished read: verdict sentence, a
gradient-bordered card labeled "FIX THIS FIRST" with a timestamped note, a
"What's working" list with green checks.
Panel 4: her deadpan-offended reaction face, overlay "okay… that's
annoyingly true".
Panel 5: her speaking to camera, overlay "It doesn't tell you how to go
viral. It just tells you the truth."
Under each panel, one small gray direction line: 1 "no intro, start
mid-motion" · 2 "screen recording, hold 3s" · 3 "pause on the fix card" ·
4 "unscripted reaction, single take" · 5 "hard cut on the last word, no
outro". Storyboard style: photorealistic panels inside neat rounded
rectangles, printed shot-card layout, all text perfectly legible.

### Shoot-day notes (not for the image model)

  read from the live app. If her actual read says something different, the
  on-screen text in beats 3–4 changes to match reality — never fake a read.
- The honesty rules from LAUNCH_KIT.md govern the published video: never
  claim account growth, never fake the reaction, show it when the app gets
  one wrong.
